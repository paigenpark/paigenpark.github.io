I am a PhD candidate in Demography at the University of California, Berkeley, where I also earned an MA in Statistics.

My research career began with sociological analyses of community change, rurality, and immigrant outcomes in the US, leading to solo and co‑authored articles in journals such as Population Research and Policy Review and Sustainability. I’ve examined how gender, immigration status, and policy context shape life outcomes, and more recently, I co‑authored work on employer responses to paid‑leave policies during COVID‑19.

My dissertation work lies at the intersection of demography, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. I am particularly interested in how deep learning can improve demographic forecasting, and how natural language processing can be used to extract demographic insights from large text corpora. My current projects include developing deep learning models and explainability techniques for mortality forecasting and using advanced NLP to analyze the content of Demographic and Health Surveys (DHS) research articles over time.
